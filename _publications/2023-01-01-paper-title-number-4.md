The project involved shortlisting five major targetable proteins of the SARS-CoV-2 virus and computationally investigating the binding affinity of 17 NSAIDs against the wild type and mutated targets.


RNA viruses are susceptible to mutations thus generating mutated strains. The investigation of NSAIDs against both protein types indicate a potent drug candidate that have similar binding affinities to both.
