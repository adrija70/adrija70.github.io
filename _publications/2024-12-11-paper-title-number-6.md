The project focused on investigating estrogen derivatives as potential cancer treatments and distinguishing the underlying mechanisms through which they act.

Through in silico and in vitro analyses, we validated that 2-methoxy estradiol and its derivative, oxime, bind to tubulin in a differential, entropy-driven manner. 
This indicated that structural variations in estrogen derivatives, such as Oxime, can enhance anti-mitotic potential. 

These findings suggested that oxime could serve as a viable colchicine site inhibitor (CSI), potentially overcoming the limitations of existing tubulin-targeting therapies.
